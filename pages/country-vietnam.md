**When:** 2017–2018

**Purpose:** Support sustainable growth, environmental protection, natural-resource management, and proactive climate response through a national CLEWs model, macroeconomic forecasting, and stronger inter-institutional analysis.

**What happened:** Viet Nam's CLEWs project followed introductory workshops in 2017 and 2018 with training workshops in December 2018 and March 2019. The completion plan focused on finalizing the model scope, generating scenario matrices, preparing policy analysis, and disseminating results to national stakeholders.

**Key insights:**

- The project centered on NDC and mitigation pathways, water security, land-use implications, climate resilience, and regional stress points such as Mekong Delta saline intrusion and Central Highlands water stress.
- A spatial water-energy-food model represented Viet Nam through northern, central, and southern regions, with power technologies such as coal, hydro, gas, solar, and wind.
- The clustering-based model structure included 512 technologies, 440 commodities, and six time slices per year, combining two seasons with three parts of the day.
- Priority crops included cassava, coffee, maize, paddy rice, sugarcane, vegetables, and fruits, with rainfed and irrigated systems represented under low, intermediate, and high management.
- The publication material shows how management and irrigation can transform yields: cassava rises from about 3 t/ha under rainfed low management to roughly 10 t/ha under irrigated high management.
- Project planning targeted a 95% reduction in matrix-generation time, improving the speed at which national teams could move from data updates to policy scenarios.

**Models:** CLEWs, LINK, OSeMOSYS

**Stakeholders:** Ministry of Planning and Investment, National Centre for Socio-Economic Information and Forecast, Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment, Institute of Strategy and Policy on Natural Resources and Environment, Development Strategy Institute, National Center for Hydro-Meteorological Forecasting, Ho Chi Minh City stakeholders, UNDP, UN DESA, and IAEA.
