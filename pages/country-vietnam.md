**When:** 2017–2018

**Purpose:** Support **sustainable growth, environmental protection, natural-resource management, and proactive climate response**.

**What happened:** The project centered on **NDC & mitigation pathways, water security, land-use implications, climate resilience, and water stress points** such as Mekong Delta saline intrusion or the Central Highlands. A spatial water-energy-food model represented Viet Nam through northern, central, and southern regions. Priority crops included cassava, coffee, maize, paddy rice, sugarcane, vegetables, and fruits, with rainfed & irrigated systems represented under low, intermediate, and high management.

**Key insights:**

- **Management & irrigation can transform yields**: for example, cassava production triples from 3 t/ha under rainfed, low management to 10 t/ha under irrigated, high management.

**Models:**  LINK, OSeMOSYS

**Stakeholders:** Ministry of Planning & Investment, National Centre for Socio-Economic Information & Forecast, Ministry of Natural Resources & Environment, Institute of Strategy & Policy on Natural Resources & Environment, Development Strategy Institute, National Center for Hydro-Meteorological Forecasting, Ho Chi Minh City stakeholders, UN DESA, UNDP, IAEA.
