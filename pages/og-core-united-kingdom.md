**When:** 2023–2026

**Purpose:** Provide a United Kingdom calibration of OG-Core for dynamic general equilibrium analysis of national fiscal policy, using data sources such as ONS, OBR, Bank of England, and GOV.UK.

**What happened:** The public example runs baseline and reform simulations in steady state and transition path modes. The reform raises the **basic income tax rate from 20% to 21%** starting on 1 January 2026. The example can be run in one-sector or eight-sector mode and can vary the age-specific treatment of households.

**Key insights:** OG-UK translates model output into real-world pound-denominated impacts, reporting changes in **GDP**, **consumption**, **investment**, **government spending**, **tax revenue**, **debt**, and the interest rate. The transition-path example prints year-by-year reform impacts for the first decade and selected later years, which makes short-run adjustment and long-run effects easier to compare.

**Resources:** [Repository](https://github.com/PSLmodels/OG-UK) and [documentation](https://pslmodels.github.io/OG-UK/).

**Models:** OG-Core

**Stakeholders:** PSLmodels, Jason DeBacker, Richard W. Evans, Jonathan Pycroft, PolicyEngine UK, ONS, OBR, Bank of England, and GOV.UK.
