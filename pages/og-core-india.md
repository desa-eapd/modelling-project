**When:** 2023–2026

**Purpose:** Provide an India-specific OG-Core calibration for dynamic general equilibrium analysis of fiscal policy, including how tax and spending reforms affect macroeconomic aggregates, labor, capital, wages, interest rates, and revenues over time.

**What happened:** The public repository runs a baseline and a reform simulation. The reform example combines a **personal income tax reform** with standard deduction, rebate threshold, and rebate ceiling changes, together with a **corporate income tax rate of 35%**. The workflow estimates tax functions, solves the baseline and reform transition paths, and produces percentage-change output for the first ten years and the steady state.

**Key insights:** The India implementation is set up to quantify how personal and corporate tax reforms affect macroeconomic outcomes over time. The repository generates comparison tables and key graphs for **GDP**, **consumption**, **capital**, **labor**, **interest rates**, and **wages**, allowing analysts to compare short-run transition effects with long-run steady-state impacts.

**Resources:** [Repository](https://github.com/Revenue-Academy/OG-IND) and [documentation](https://revenue-academy.github.io/OG-IND/).

**Models:** OG-Core
