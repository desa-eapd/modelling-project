**When:** 2016–2019

**Purpose:** Explore strategies for sustainable development in the **food-energy-water nexus**, including agriculture, water competition, bioenergy, energy security, and affordability. The available modelling report developed Ghana's energy-system block as a foundation for a larger CLEWs assessment.

**What happened:** An OSeMOSYS model was built for the national energy sector with 2016 as the base year and a 2016-2050 modelling horizon. It represented agricultural, commercial, industrial, residential, and transport demand and produced preliminary power-generation, import, and cost results.

**Key insights:**

- Ghana's 2016 energy balance in the model has 9,598.4 ktoe of total energy supply and 7,085.2 ktoe of final energy consumption.
- Final consumption in 2016 is led by residential demand at 3,119.0 ktoe and transport at 2,642.8 ktoe, followed by industry at 862.9 ktoe, commercial demand at 357.0 ktoe, and agriculture and fisheries at 103.5 ktoe.
- Preliminary results grow total generation capacity from 4.8 GW in 2018 to 10.5 GW in 2050, with hydro fixed around 1.6 GW and gas and coal taking larger roles in the least-cost solution.
- Energy imports are dominated by petroleum products across the modelling horizon, representing around 75% of imports in energy terms; LNG accounts for most of the remainder.
- Electricity generation costs are about USD 56/MWh in 2018, rise to around USD 60/MWh in 2020, and stabilize near USD 50/MWh by 2050 as the fuel-cost share falls from about 75% to around 50%.
