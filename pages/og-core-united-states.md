**When:** 2023–2026

**Purpose:** Provide the U.S. calibration of OG-Core for dynamic general equilibrium analysis of federal fiscal policy, including macroeconomic, revenue, wage, interest-rate, and distributionally relevant effects over time.

**What happened:** The public repository runs a baseline and reform transition path. The example calibrates tax functions with Tax-Calculator inputs and compares the baseline with a reform that sets the **corporate income tax rate to 35%**. The run produces summary percentage changes, macro time-series output, and plots/tables comparing the baseline and reform.

**Key insights:** OG-USA is set up to evaluate how tax reforms affect GDP, investment, consumption, labor, capital, wages, interest rates, and tax revenue across both transition paths and steady states. Key repository graphs and tables include **macro time-series output**, **GDP**, **consumption**, **capital**, **labor**, **interest rates**, and **wages**.

**Resources:** [Repository](https://github.com/PSLmodels/OG-USA) and [documentation](https://pslmodels.github.io/OG-USA/).

**Models:** OG-Core

**Stakeholders:** PSLmodels, Jason DeBacker, Richard W. Evans, PSL Foundation, Open Research Group, and Tax-Calculator.
