**When:** 2019-2021

**Purpose:** Support Indonesia's review of national energy policy and renewable-energy targets by making climate, land, energy, and water interactions visible in one modelling framework.

**What happened:** Following a request from the Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources, UN DESA and partners scoped a national CLEWs application in Jakarta, developed a first country model, and ran training workshops with national experts. A second phase focused on scenario analysis for renewable-energy targets, NDC alignment, and sustainable-development strategies.

**Key insights:**

- The project was designed as an interministerial exercise, with energy, agriculture, environment and forests, finance, and related agencies expected to participate in model development and scenario review.
- The first phase developed a tailored national CLEWs model and delivered two training workshops between late 2019 and early 2020.
- Biodiesel result tables show how higher blending ambition shifts fuel demand from diesel to FAME. In the extracted model runs, B40 raises FAME demand from 386.5 to 515.3 in 2025 and from 479.7 to 639.6 in 2030, while B50 reaches 799.5 in 2030.
- The co-firing scenario material tests coal-biomass substitution, including biomass shares rising from 1% in 2020 to 3% in 2021 and 5% in 2023 in the scenario assumptions.
- The CLEWs structure gives Indonesia a way to test whether biofuel, co-firing, land-use, and renewable-power goals can be pursued together without moving pressure from one system to another.

**Resources:** Project documents, workshop minutes, co-firing scenario material, and B40/B50 model result tables.

**Models:** CLEWs, OSeMOSYS

**Stakeholders:** Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources, Directorate General of New, Renewable Energy and Energy Conservation, sector ministries and agencies, UNDP Indonesia, UNDP Bangkok Regional Hub, UN DESA, and KTH.
