UN DESA, in collaboration with Ethiopia’s Policy Studies Institute (PSI), and with support from the United Nations Resident Coordinator’s Office (UNRCO) in Ethiopia and the United Nations Economic Commission for Africa (ECA), will host a five-day advanced training workshop on the Overlapping Generations Macroeconomy Model for Ethiopia (OG-ETH). The workshop will take place at the United Nations Conference Centre in Addis Ababa from 11 to 15 May 2026. 

This initiative forms part of a broader effort to strengthen the capacity of national institutions to use data and analytical tools to inform policy decisions and support sustainable recovery strategies. 

The workshop aims to deepen the technical capacity of government officials, researchers, and academic participants to apply the OG-ETH model for policy analysis and planning. Building on the inception workshop held in November 2025, this second training will focus on advanced aspects of the model, including its technical foundations, integration of Ethiopian national data, and simulation of priority policy reforms. 

Throughout the workshop, participants will engage in hands-on sessions covering model calibration, collaborative data and code management, interpretation of simulation results, and the formulation of policy recommendations. The workshop will also contribute to building a national network of experts to update and further develop the model, ensuring capacities are sustained after the project ends.  

Participants will include representatives from key national institutions such as the Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Planning and Development, National Bank of Ethiopia, Capital Market Authority, Policy Studies Institute, Ethiopian Economics Association, and academic institutions, as well as partners from UNECA, UNRCO, and the UN Country Team in Ethiopia. 

For more information, please visit [OG-CORE](https://desa-eapd.github.io/modelling-project/og-core/) model page.
